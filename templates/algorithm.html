Identify Under-performing Cameras

Establish Camera-monitoring Area

Each crash event can be assigned to a closest camera, based on the geographical distance. By performing this procedure for each crash, I established a monitoring area for every camera. For a certain time period, I have the information on number of violations recorded by a certain camera, and the number of crashes happened in its monitoring area.


Mark High-risk Areas

The ratio of crash counts vs. violation counts was calculated for each camera. High-risk areas were defined as monitoring areas with under-performing cameras, that show a relatively high ratio.


Install New Cameras

Camera relocation are/or new camera installation are recommended for high-risk areas. Within the monitoring area of each under-performing camera, the new camera should be located at centroids of crash events. It appears like a common clustering problem. A general k-means algorithm should be sufficient to find new-camera installation sites. However, crashes only occur along streets, k-means sometimes locate centroids off-street. To solve this problem, a "smarter" clustering method should be applied.

HDBSCAN is a clustering method dealing with spatially highly skewed data. It could potentially recognize crashes along the same street a cluster. By combining main street information from shape files, I can compute a crash center for each street segment.





Evaluate New-camera Effects

Estimate Installation Date

To estimate the new camera effect on reducing crash rate, I compared crash counts before and after the new camera installation. The installation date was not given in my data set, but I can estimate it from day 1 of the violation report for each camera.

Compare with City Average

The comparison of before- and after-crash counts need to be put in a context. In other words, we need to evalute crash risk comparing to the city average. I normalized the crash risk of new-camera areas, assuming the city average to be 1.

In February 2015, there are three new speed cameras installed in the city of Chicago. A significant 30% crash risk reduction was observed within the first week after the camera installation. And the reduced crash risk remained the same low last for at least seven weeks. What does it mean by 30% crash risk reduction? In 2015, there are 21667 injuried and 119 fatalities. A 30% crash risk reduction means 6500 less injuried and 36 survived. It is quite significant.
