Improving Road Safety Using Camera Violation and Crash Data for the city of Chicago

Project Goals

Efficient Police Deployment

Traffic rule enforcement is one of the core duties of the Police Department. However, the police resource is limited, how to make efficient deployment while maximizing road safety is a key question. This project provide a tool to predict locations throughout the city where traffic violations could be potentially high in the near future. By placing police officers at these selected locations, we hope to achieve:

a) Promote the change to a good-driving behavior
b) Discover underlying reason for the violation, which cameras could not provide
c) Avoid the waiting period of a mailed ticket, so that traffic rule offenders will not forget the cause of their violations

Data-driven City Planning

Cameras are usually installed by the Department of City Planning. Installation at a major intersection or on a busy street is a common choice. How to smartly select a camera installation site is difficult to achieve.

By combing the crash record together with the information of current camera sites, we propose to use a data-driven approach to suggest next camera installation areas and evaluate the impact of some newly installed speeding camera on the number of crashes in the neighborhood.
